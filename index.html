Messaging Approach: Universal with Mid-Sequence Branching

We do not know which FA system any of these institutions is currently running. The WSCUC directory does not contain that information, and the existing HubSpot data is not being used. Rather than guessing, we use a single universal sequence that works regardless of the contact's current system, then branch based on what BDRs learn through calls and replies.

Strategic Rationale

01

Directory-only list to eliminate customer outreach risk

The existing HubSpot FA list of 4,134 contacts includes current Portico customers, with no reliable way to filter them out today. Sending cold outbound to existing customers is damaging to the relationship and undermines trust with accounts the team is already managing. The WSCUC directory is a clean, bounded list with no overlap risk and no dependency on HubSpot data quality.

02

Universal message because we do not have system data

The WSCUC directory contains no information about which FA system each institution uses. Building two pre-segmented sequences without that data would mean guessing, which produces inconsistent and sometimes wrong messaging. A universal sequence that asks "is your system ready?" works for every institution regardless of what they are running, and surfaces the right information naturally through calls and replies for mid-sequence branching.

03

Executive-level messaging, not FA operations copy

The WSCUC directory contacts are Presidents, Provosts, VPs Finance, and ALOs, not FA Directors. Messaging that leads with staff workload and calculation complexity does not land with this audience. The right frame is institutional compliance risk and accreditation posture: OB3 is a federal mandate that affects eligibility, audit exposure, and ultimately enrollment. That is a conversation executives care about.

04

Calls at Days 5 and 14 serve two purposes

The Day 5 call is not primarily a conversion attempt: it is an intelligence-gathering step. Learning what FA system the institution runs allows the BDR to move the contact into the right branch for Emails 5-7. The Day 14 call is a direct follow-up for contacts who have not replied across four touches. Together, they surface the highest-intent contacts and provide the segmentation data the sequence needs to sharpen in its second half.

05

$500 LinkedIn as a sequence multiplier, not a standalone channel

At $500 targeting a 300-person audience, the LinkedIn campaign cannot generate meaningful pipeline on its own. Its value is name recognition: contacts who have seen a Portico OB3 ad before an email arrives are more likely to open it and more likely to respond. This warming effect on cold outbound sequences is measurable and makes the fixed budget significantly more efficient than using either channel alone.
